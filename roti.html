Roti

Roti is made from stoneground whole wheat flour, traditionally known as gehu ka atta, and water that is combined into a dough.Its defining characteristic is that it is unleavened.

Wheat being a staple in the Northern parts of India, rotis, chapatis, and phulkas are the mainstay.

Calories

The number of calories in a roti depends on its size and the amount of oil or ghee used:

Medium roti:

A medium-sized roti of about 40 g has 120 calories.

Large roti:

A large roti has 137 calories.

Extra large roti:

An extra large roti of 25 cm has 185 calories.

Wheat roti:

A small wheat roti has 90 calories, while a larger one can have up to 110 calories.
