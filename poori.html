Poori

Puri (sometimes spelled as poori) is a deep-fried bread made from unleavened whole-wheat flour that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is eaten for breakfast or as a snack or light meal. It is usually served with a savory curry or bhaji, as in puri bhaji, but may also be eaten with sweet dishes.

Wheat being a staple in the Northern parts of India, rotis, chapatis, and phulkas are the mainstay.

Calories

A poori has 144 calories. It also contains:

  • 9.8g total fat
  • 0.8g saturated fat
  • 0.2g trans fat
  • 6.3g polyunsaturated fat
  • 2.2g monounsaturated fat
  • 0mg cholesterol
  • 85mg sodium

A poori provides about 5 percent of the total daily calorie requirement of a standard adult diet of 2,000 calories
The calorie breakdown of 100 grams of fried whole wheat poori is: 28% fat, 62% carbs, 10% prot

Note:

*****

If you are trying to lose weight or maintain a healthy weight, you may want to limit yourself to 2-3 puris per meal.
