IDLY

Idli Sambhar is typically known as South Indian food. But it is truly believed that once you start having this as regular breakfast, any person irrespective of his native belongingness will start loving it. This healthy breakfast option is loaded with numerous health benefits, is easy to cook, digest, lose weight, easy to count your calories, loaded with iron and contains a good amount of fibre. It is oil-free, and steamed in a such a proportion so as to serve you delicious meal and make your stomach full for longer. It has more than 185 different varieties loaded with flavours and different tastes.

Idli is old as its origin dates back to 2000 years. Idli is generally accompanied by Sambhar and chutney. But Idli-Sambhar combination is nothing less then Chole-Bhature, Chole-Chawal, Rajma-Chawal, Puri-Aloo, Parantha-makhan, etc. Sambhar is again cooked in very less oil loaded with lentils and vegetables with a blend of Indian spices, which is the healthiest and keeps you at the best of health.

Calories

The nutrient value of a single medium-sized (regular) idli is: 35-39 calories,
2-3 grams of protein,
2-5 grams of dietary fibre and 6-10 grams of carbohydrates;
1-5 milligram of iron,
and trace amounts of calcium, folate, potassium, and vitamin A
depending on the flavour item used in it.
